Biden's Consumer Watchdog Pushes for Last-Minute Stablecoin Rule
----------------------------------------------------------------

Date : 21:22 10 Jan 2025

The US Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) has proposed new regulations that would impact stablecoin issuers and wallet providers. The proposal seeks to apply the Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) to virtual wallets and stablecoins, which are digital tokens tied to the value of a steady asset, such as the US dollar. This would require stablecoin firms and crypto wallet providers to follow consumer protection regulations, including making disclosures and providing protections against unauthorized transactions.

The proposal is at an early stage, and its fate is uncertain given the upcoming arrival of President-elect Donald Trump, who will have the power to appoint a new CFPB chief. The current director, Rohit Chopra, appears unlikely to step down voluntarily, but Trump can fire him or replace him with someone else.

The proposed regulations would treat stablecoins as a payment mechanism and apply consumer protections to them, including requiring institutions that provide virtual currency wallets to make disclosures and provide protections against unauthorized transactions. This could impact decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms, which often operate without outside interference or use self-hosted wallet technology.

Some advocates, such as Better Markets, support the proposal, arguing it would protect consumers and level the playing field among digital payment mechanisms. However, others, including the Cato Institute's Jack Solowey, have criticized the proposal, saying it doesn't adequately address decentralized ledgers and self-hosted wallets.

The proposed regulations are currently open for public comment, but their future is uncertain given the incoming Trump administration and the possibility that a Republican-led Congress could erase them using the Congressional Review Act.

The project utilizes BitVM2, a computing paradigm that enables Ethereum-style smart contracts on Bitcoin, paving the way for zero-knowledge technology. Zero-knowledge computations enable parties to verify information without revealing what it is.

The goal of this initiative is to unlock the value stored in BTC and allow it to be transacted on other blockchains that may not have the same limitations as Bitcoin. This is part of a broader movement to extract greater utility from Bitcoin, similar to what happens on networks like Ethereum.

Babylon's staking protocol has already gained significant traction, with a total value locked (TVL) of around $5.5 billion. The introduction of BitVM2's predecessor was hailed as a breakthrough for making Bitcoin more programmable, and now developers are building bridges between Bitcoin and other networks using this technology.

One example is Citrea, a zero-knowledge rollup backed by Galaxy Digital, which deployed a BitVM-based bridge to the Bitcoin testnet. This bridge aims to be compatible with the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM), enabling smart contracts execution on both platforms.

Bitcoin Funding Rates Briefly Went Negative, Usually Marks a Local Bottom: Van Straten
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Date : 10:26 10 Jan 2025

The article discusses the recent volatility in Bitcoin's (BTC) price, which has been swinging between $90,000 and $100,000 since November 18th. The author notes that when BTC approaches $100,000, investors tend to become bullish, while approaching $90,000 leads to bearish sentiment.

The article highlights the role of derivatives, such as futures and options, in influencing the price swings. One key metric is the perpetual funding rate, which measures the average rate set by exchanges for perpetual futures contracts. A positive rate indicates that long positions are paying short positions, while a negative rate suggests the opposite.

As of Thursday, the funding rate briefly went into negative territory (-0.001%), marking its first occurrence this year and only the second time since November. This led to a "leverage flush" and a shift in sentiment before BTC returned above $94,000.

The article notes that negative funding rates can signal a possible price rebound or bottom, but also caution that they don't always lead to immediate price movements. The author suggests that positive funding rates during a bull market may not necessarily mean the market is overheated, while brief periods of negative rates during a bear market could indicate a continued downturn.

The article concludes by stating that when the funding rate goes negative, it often signals the emergence of a floor in the market, as bears become overconfident and get liquidated. Similarly, when bulls become complacent, the spot price can no longer keep up with the leverage used, leading to a correction.
